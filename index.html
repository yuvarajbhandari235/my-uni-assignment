05. Critical reflection

Reflection on skill attainment

The reflection follows a clear progression from experience to analysis, learning, and future action.

Assessment Task 3 350 words

At the beginning of this module, I understood emotional intelligence and leadership mainly as personal qualities. Through the EcoBin project and the leadership and employability event, I learned that both skills are observable professional behaviours that influence group performance. Emotional intelligence involves recognising emotions, understanding their effect on decisions, and responding appropriately (Mayer, Salovey and Caruso, 2004). Leadership involves influencing a group towards a shared purpose while supporting relationships and task completion (Northouse, 2021).

My emotional intelligence developed when a teammate misunderstood my instructions and became upset. Instead of defending my position immediately, I remained calm, listened to their concern, and explained the intended task more clearly. This reduced tension and allowed us to continue working productively. I also supported a colleague who was experiencing pressure from a heavy workload. By helping with selected tasks and offering encouragement, I contributed to timely completion and a more positive working atmosphere. These experiences showed me that empathy is most valuable when it leads to respectful and practical action.

My people management and leadership developed through my role as Digital Strategy and Accountability Lead for EcoBin. I helped maintain awareness of the 15 July 2026 deadline, supported progress across the digital work, and contributed to decisions about the university target market and pricing structure. The team completed the presentation on time. Tutor feedback indicated that the idea was communicated well, although some areas required deeper analysis. I accepted this feedback as evidence that meeting a deadline is not the same as achieving the strongest possible quality. Effective leadership therefore requires both delivery discipline and critical challenge.

The event on 29 November 2025 strengthened my understanding of communication, adaptability, diversity, and continuous learning. It helped me connect academic ideas with workplace expectations. My next development priority is to ask more analytical questions during group work, record actions after meetings, and invite feedback before final submission. This will help me become a more reflective manager who combines empathy, accountability, and evidence-based decision-making. I will measure improvement through clearer task records, earlier peer reviews, and evidence that team discussions lead to stronger, more thoroughly justified decisions.

Reflective focus Experience → analysis → learning → action
